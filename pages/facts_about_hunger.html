Facts About Hunger in North Arkansas

Hunger is a hidden crisis that affects many in our community. At The Breadbasket of North Arkansas, we believe knowledge is power. Understanding the realities of hunger can inspire action and support for those in need. Here are some important facts and statistics about hunger in our area and beyond.

Quick Facts

  • 1 in 6 individuals in North Arkansas faces food insecurity, not knowing where their next meal will come from.
  • Children are disproportionately affected, with about 1 in 4 struggling with hunger, impacting their health and ability to learn.
  • Seniors on fixed incomes often have to choose between paying for healthcare or buying groceries.

Understanding Hunger

Hunger does not just stem from a lack of food, but from a complex web of factors, including poverty, unemployment, and insufficient access to healthy food. It affects individuals and families in both urban and rural areas, creating a cycle that's hard to break without support.

Who Is Hungry?

  • Families: Many working families earn too much to qualify for government assistance but too little to meet all their expenses, including food.
  • Children: Kids in food-insecure homes may face developmental impairments and struggle academically.
  • Elderly: Seniors may have to choose between medication and food, compromising their health.

How We Help

The Breadbasket of North Arkansas works tirelessly to address hunger by:

  • Distributing nutritious food to those in need.
  • Educating the community on nutrition and food security.
  • Advocating for policy changes to address the root causes of hunger.

Join the Fight Against Hunger!

Understanding these facts is the first step in making a difference. Your support through donations, volunteering, or spreading awareness can help change lives. Together, we can work towards a future where no one goes hungry.
